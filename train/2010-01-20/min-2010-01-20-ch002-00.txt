2. Announcement by the President

The President announced that he had received a letter from the  President
of the European Council, Herman van Rompuy, informing him of the Council's
decision to consult the European Parliament on the proposal by the Spanish
Government concerning the composition of the European Parliament, and to
seek Parliament's consent not to convene a Convention on this matter.

This proposal had been sent to the committee responsible, the AFCO
Committee, which would appoint a rapporteur.

                                      
                                      

The President announced that the Government of Bulgaria had decided to
withdraw its candidate for Commissioner-designate and had put forward a new
candidate. The Conference of Presidents would decide the next day, Thursday
21 January 2010, on the timetable for the new hearing and the vote on
approving the new Commission.

                                      
                                      

The President explained the arrangements governing cooperation with the
Council Presidency following the entry into force of the Lisbon Treaty.
Each rotating Presidency would present an action programme at the beginning
of its term in office and a final report at the end. The President of the
European Council would present the results of the European Summits.
