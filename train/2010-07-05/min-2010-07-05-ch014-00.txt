14. Order of business

The next item was the order of business.
The final draft agenda for the July sittings (PE 444.569/PDOJ) had been
distributed and a number of changes had been proposed (Rule 140):

Monday
Request by the Verts/ALE and S&D Groups to hold over until a subsequent
part-session the debate on the report by Marielle Gallo on the enforcement
of intellectual property rights in the internal market (A7-0175/2010).

The following spoke: Daniel Cohn-Bendit, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group,
who moved the request, Martin Schulz, on behalf of the S&D Group, in
support of the request, and Manfred Weber, on behalf of the PPE Group,
against the request.

Parliament agreed to the request by electronic vote (140 in favour, 135
against, 1 abstention).

Tuesday

No changes.
Wednesday

No changes.
Thursday

Request by the ECR Group to change the order of the debates on cases of
breaches of human rights, democracy and the rule of law (Rule 122) as
follows:

- Zimbabwe (item 164 on the final draft agenda)

- Venezuela (item 163 on the final draft agenda)

- North Korea (item 165 on the final draft agenda)

The following spoke: Martin Callanan, on behalf of the ECR Group, who moved
the request, Francesco Enrico Speroni, on behalf of the EFD Group, in
support of the request, Martin Schulz and Martin Callanan.

Parliament agreed to the request.

The order of business was thus established.
