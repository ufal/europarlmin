13. Order of business

The next item was the order of business.
The final draft agenda for the May 2009 sittings (PE 423.628 PDOJ) had been
distributed and a number of changes had been proposed (Rule 132):

Monday, Tuesday

No changes.
Wednesday

Request by the GUE/NGL Group to remove from the agenda the joint debate on
the Treaty of Lisbon (items 64, 65, 66, 67 and 68 on the final draft
agenda).

The following spoke: Mary Lou McDonald, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, who
moved the request, and Daniel Cohn-Bendit, on behalf of the Verts/ALE
Group.

By roll-call vote (11 in favour, 147 against, 4 abstentions), Parliament
rejected the request.

Request by the GUE/NGL Group to place on the agenda a Commission statement
on the green paper on the common fisheries policy.

The following spoke: Pedro Guerreiro, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, who
moved the request, and Philippe Morillon, Chair of the PECH Committee.

Parliament rejected the request.
Thursday

Debate on cases of breaches of human rights, democracy and the rule of law
(Rule 115):

Request by the PPE-DE Group to replace the item 'Fiji' (item 82 on the
final draft agenda) with an item entitled 'Iran: the case of Roxana
Saberi'.

Request by the GUE/NGL Group to replace the item 'Venezuela: the case of
Manuel Rosales' (item 84 on the final draft agenda) with an item entitled
'Iran: the case of Roxana Saberi'.

Parliament agreed to the PPE-DE Group's request by electronic vote (106 in
favour, 57 against, 3 abstentions).

The request by the GUE/NGL Group lapsed.

                                      
                                      

The order of business was thus established.
